1. Elephants

Description: Elephants are large, intelligent mammals native to Africa and Asia, known for their strong social bonds and long trunks.

Diet: Grasses (stems and blades), leaves and bark from trees like acacia, fruits (pulp and seeds).

Life Span: 60–70 years in the wild.

Conditions: Parasites, skin infections, joint pain.

Medicinal Plants:

  • Acacia: Tannins fight parasites; consumed via leaves and bark.
  • Baobab: Antibacterial, anti-inflammatory; eaten as leaves, bark, and fruit pulp for skin and joint health.
  • Marula: Anti-inflammatory; fruit pulp and leaves aid joint pain and healing.

Elephants roam with wisdom, using plants to shield their massive bodies from harm.
