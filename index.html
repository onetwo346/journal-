1. Elephants

Diet: Grasses, leaves, bark, fruits (up to 300 lbs daily).

Medicinal Plants:

  • Neem (Azadirachta indica): Antibacterial, antifungal; combats parasites, skin ailments, acts as a dewormer.
  • Baobab (Adansonia digitata): High in vitamin C and antioxidants; boosts immunity and healing.
  • Marula (Sclerocarya birrea): Anti-inflammatory; aids digestion and vitality.

Elephants’ massive intake of medicinal plants helps sustain their size and resilience, acting as a natural shield against infections and environmental stress.

2. Rhinoceroses

Diet: Grasses, leaves, shrubs, bark.

Medicinal Plants:

  • Moringa (Moringa oleifera): Antioxidant, anti-inflammatory; supports joints and reduces stress.
  • Aloe Vera: Anti-inflammatory; heals wounds and aids digestion.
  • Devil’s Claw (Harpagophytum procumbens): Anti-inflammatory; eases arthritis and joint pain.

Rhinos’ tough diet includes plants that fortify their thick skin and massive frames, offering natural relief from inflammation and injury.

3. Bison

Diet: Grasses, herbs, shrubs.

Medicinal Plants:

  • Echinacea (Echinacea purpurea): Immune-boosting; prevents infections.
  • Yarrow (Achillea millefolium): Wound-healing; aids clotting and repair.
  • Dandelion (Taraxacum officinale): Detoxifies liver, regulates digestion.

Bison roam vast plains, grazing on plants that keep their herds strong and healthy through natural immunity and repair mechanisms.

4. Hippopotamuses

Diet: Grasses, aquatic plants, fruits.

Medicinal Plants:

  • Water Hyacinth (Eichhornia crassipes): Anti-inflammatory, antioxidant; reduces skin irritation.
  • Cattails (Typha): Soothes digestion, treats stomach issues.
  • Papyrus (Cyperus papyrus): Antibacterial; cleanses digestive system.

Hippos’ semi-aquatic lifestyle pairs perfectly with plants that heal their skin and digestion, thriving in their riverine habitats.

Gorillas’ forest diet includes herbs that soothe their robust digestive systems and calm their social lifestyles.
